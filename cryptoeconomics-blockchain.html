Blockchain is a topic where enthusiasm has consistently outpaced understanding. This course is designed to close that gap. We begin with the mathematics and cryptography that make these systems work — hash functions, public-key cryptography, consensus mechanisms — because without that foundation, everything that follows is just vocabulary. From there the course moves through Ethereum, smart contracts, DeFi, and NFTs with the same rigour applied to the fundamentals.

The failures in this space are studied as carefully as the successes. The Terra/Luna collapse, flash loan exploits, smart contract vulnerabilities — these are not footnotes but precise illustrations of what happens when economic incentives are designed without a full understanding of the system they operate in. The regulatory landscape receives the same serious attention: Swiss FINMA guidelines, EU MiCA compliance, and the trajectory of CBDC development are treated as core material, not background reading.

Four hands-on workshops close the course because there is no substitute for building the thing yourself. Students deploy smart contracts, create a cryptocurrency, mint NFTs with IPFS-hosted metadata, and build a functioning DAO — not as guided demonstrations, but as projects they own and can fully explain. That practical experience is what gives the theoretical knowledge somewhere to land.
