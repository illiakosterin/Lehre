Cryptoeconomics & Blockchain is a 12-week course that takes students from the mathematical foundations of cryptography all the way through to live smart contract deployment and regulatory compliance. It treats blockchain not as a single technology but as an ecosystem — covering Bitcoin, Ethereum, DeFi protocols, NFT markets, tokenomics, and the layer-2 scaling solutions that make these systems viable at scale.

The course emphasises understanding over memorisation. Students learn why consensus mechanisms work the way they do, how economic incentives are embedded in protocol design, and what happens when those incentives break down — using real case studies like the Terra/Luna collapse and flash loan exploits to make the analysis concrete. The regulatory landscape is treated with equal seriousness, with dedicated coverage of Swiss FINMA guidelines, EU MiCA compliance, and the global trajectory of digital asset law.

Four hands-on workshops in Module H move beyond theory entirely. Students deploy their own smart contracts on Ethereum, create and launch a cryptocurrency, mint NFTs with IPFS-hosted metadata, and build a functioning DAO — giving them a practical portfolio of blockchain work by the end of the course.
