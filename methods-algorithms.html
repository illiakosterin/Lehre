At the MSc level, it is not sufficient to know which function to call. Students are expected to understand what is happening inside the model — the geometry of a decision boundary, the assumptions embedded in a loss function, the reason a random forest behaves differently from a single tree. That depth of understanding is what makes it possible to adapt when the standard approach does not work, which in applied work is common.

The course covers six topics — regression, logistic regression, KNN and clustering, random forests, dimensionality reduction, and reinforcement learning — going deep on each rather than surveying many superficially. Every topic is connected to finance: return prediction, portfolio segmentation, compression of high-dimensional market data, agents that learn from price signals. The objective is not a broad familiarity with algorithm names, but a working command of a focused set of methods.

The group assignment carries 60% of the grade because applying methods to a self-sourced dataset — choosing the right approach, validating the results, and articulating what was found — reflects what research and industry practice actually look like. Each lecture is also available in two formats: a full technical treatment with mathematical detail, and an accessible version that builds intuition without sacrificing rigour. Both are available to every student.
