Methods & Algorithms is a graduate-level course that builds rigorous understanding of the core machine learning techniques used in data science and quantitative finance. Rather than surveying a broad catalogue of models, the course goes deep on six foundational topics — ensuring students understand not just how to apply an algorithm, but why it works, where it breaks down, and how to tune it for real data.

Finance applications run throughout. Regression models are framed around return prediction and risk factor analysis; clustering methods are applied to portfolio segmentation; dimensionality reduction techniques are used on high-dimensional asset data. This grounding makes the material immediately relevant for students heading into quantitative roles in asset management, risk, or data-driven research.

Each lecture is delivered in two formats — a technical deep-dive for students who want full mathematical rigour, and an accessible version that builds intuition without sacrificing accuracy. Both ship alongside a Colab notebook with a synthetic dataset, so every concept can be explored hands-on. The group assignment, worth 60% of the grade, asks students to apply five of the six topics to a self-sourced dataset — making the final deliverable a genuine piece of applied research.
