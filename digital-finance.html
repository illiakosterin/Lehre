Digital Finance bridges traditional finance education with the tools and frameworks reshaping the industry. Rather than treating technology as an add-on, the course positions it as the primary lens — examining how FinTech, blockchain, AI, and automation are restructuring everything from payments and lending to risk management and regulatory compliance.

The course is built around real-world problems rather than abstract theory. Students work through challenges that practitioners face today: how to reduce transaction costs, how to build trust in decentralised systems, how to automate compliance workflows, and how to manage risk in an environment where digital assets and algorithmic trading are mainstream. This problem-based structure ensures the material stays grounded in current industry practice.

With over 80 lessons, 161 notebooks, and 1,000+ charts, the course offers one of the most resource-dense curricula in digital finance education. Three distinct editions — classic topic-based, problem-based, and a PhD research seminar — allow the material to serve undergraduate students building foundational knowledge and doctoral researchers working at the frontier of the field.
